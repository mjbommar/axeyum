# P1.6 — Theory combination (Nelson–Oppen / interface equalities)

**Size:** M · **Depends on:** [P1.4 e-graph](P1.4-egraph.md),
[P1.5 CDCL(T)](P1.5-cdcl-t-loop.md)

## Goal
Let multiple theories share equalities so combined logics (QF_AUFLIA, QF_AUFBV,
mixed BV+arith) are decided properly rather than as stacked eager reductions.
Adopt Z3's **model-based combination**: identify shared terms, propose interface
equalities between shared terms of equal value, case-split on them.

## Why / leverage
axeyum's current composition is reduction-stacked and eager, with coupling only
propositional — there is no interface-equality propagation. This is why the
BV+LIA gap leaves `str.len` unsat as `unknown`. Combination is required before
multi-theory queries work at parity.

## 2026-07-02 — NAS `hash_sat_*` QF_UFLIA convergence: measured relocation + next slices

Target: the four NAS QF_UF `cli__regress2__hash_sat_{06_19,07_17,09_09,10_09}`
instances (declared `QF_UFLIA`, `:status sat`). Each declares 9 uninterpreted
`hash_k : Int→Int` and 7–9 integer `xᵢ` with `0 ≤ xᵢ < N`, all `xᵢ` distinct, and
for every `hash_k` the 9 values `hash_k(x₁..x₉)` are pairwise distinct **and** each
equals some `xⱼ` (i.e. each `hash_k` restricted to `{x₁..x₉}` is a **bijection** —
nine independent permutation/all-different subproblems), plus one nested-`hash`
equality. STATUS localized these as "the online interface-equality loop grinds 248
LP rounds without a sat candidate." Measurement (release, `check_auto_explained`
route trace + targeted probes) **relocates** that:

1. **The grind is the *offline* lazy-arith CEGAR, not the online interface loop.**
   `check_auto` routes these to `uf-arith-lazy-overbound`
   (`euf.rs::check_with_function_consistency` → inner
   `dpll_lia::IncrementalArithDpll::solve`). The outer function-consistency loop runs
   its inner LIA solve **once** (`solve_rounds=1`); that single inner solve grinds
   the DPLL(T) blocking-lemma loop — **633 rounds/10 s, 3245 rounds/60 s,
   `sat_candidates=0`, every core `core_src_lp`** — and never finds even one
   theory-feasible arrangement of the Ackermannized all-different abstraction. With
   **no theory propagation**, the SAT skeleton re-proposes order-cycles (from the
   `distinct`/`≠` → order-disjunction encoding) and the loop blocks one short Farkas
   cycle at a time; the arrangement space is effectively non-convergent.

2. **The online path — the one STATUS named — never runs under the default caps.**
   `check_qf_uflia_online` declines *instantly* at `MAX_BOOLEAN_ATOMS = 512`
   (`uflia_online.rs:104`); these instances have 997–1371 theory atoms.

3. **The online path is the right lever (proven).** Raising the caps
   experimentally (`MAX_BOOLEAN_ATOMS→8192`, `MAX_OPAQUE_BOOLEAN_ATOMS→4096`) lets
   the online CDCL(T) combination **solve `hash_sat_06_19` — `sat` in 7.8 s,
   replay-checked**. `07_17`/`09_09` reach a satisfying CDCL(T) leaf but the leaf
   model-rebuild fails; `10_09` times out in the boolean layer.

4. **Root cause of the leaf-rebuild grind (the actual interface-equality search).**
   At a satisfying CDCL(T) leaf, `cdclt_combined` (`uflia_online.rs` ~L1586)
   **discards the interface arrangement the online `CombinedIncrementalLia` already
   committed** and calls `decide_conjunction`, which re-runs the **full offline
   interface-arrangement DFS** (`Search::run`/`equal`/`distinct`) from scratch over
   ~1226 literals. That DFS is exponential in the interface pairs; it returns
   `"interface distinct branch inconclusive"` after consuming the entire budget on a
   *single* leaf (one leaf attempt in 8 s). This redundant re-search is the grind.

5. **`build_model` note.** The prior build_model fixes (`bff67679`, `86998ea8`)
   live in the EUF e-graph model; the residual here is the *online* leaf rebuild
   (`combined_model` + the redundant DFS) — a distinct code path.

### Experiments tried and reverted (with reasons)
- **Multi-core LP-conflict learning in `dpll_lia`** (extract up to 16 disjoint,
  individually-minimized+self-checked Farkas cores per round instead of one):
  sound and verdict-preserving, prunes ~3× faster (1856 vs 633 blocking clauses in
  10 s), but decides **none** of the four and broadly perturbs LP-round timing
  (regression risk on the `uf_arith_dispatch_differential` budget-cap balance).
  Off-target — reverted.
- **Cap raise alone:** enables the online path but yields **no decide under
  `check_auto` at the baseline 10 s budget**, because `dispatch_uf_arith_online`
  gives the online probe only *half* the budget (`probe_budget`) and `06_19` needs
  7.8 s; and a 16× cap raise reroutes many other UFLIA instances into the online
  CDCL(T) path (broad change requiring full baseline measurement). Reverted.

### Next slices (ordered)
- **Slice A (the real fix):** at the CDCL(T) leaf, **seed** the model-rebuild with
  the arrangement `CombinedIncrementalLia` already committed (interface-var truths at
  indices `atom_count..combined_count`) so `decide_conjunction`/`combined_model`
  walks *directly* to the leaf instead of re-searching the DFS. Requires aligning
  `combined.interface_pairs()` with the offline `interface_pairs(interface_terms(..))`
  ordering; keep deadline checks; replay-gated (`sat` only). Expected to flip
  `07_17`/`09_09` and make `06_19` fast. Then land a **bounded, baseline-measured**
  cap raise (curated `qf-uf`/`qf-uflia` + NAS QF_UF, DISAGREE=0).
- **Slice B:** avoid the half-budget online-probe starvation for opaque-int-app
  UFLIA where the eager lazy-arith route is known-hopeless (a cheap a-priori signal
  + baseline measurement so instances where eager wins are not starved).
- **Slice C (independent; helps the offline route broadly):** theory propagation for
  order/difference atoms in `dpll_lia` (transitivity) so the SAT skeleton never
  proposes order-cycles (`core_src_lp` is *all* cycles today); or all-different /
  finite-domain propagation for the Ackermannized permutation shape.
- **Non-goal (multi-day):** a fixed-width Int→BV reduction to the lazy-UFBV/CDCL
  path would crush the all-different combinatorics, but needs a general sound Int→BV
  lowering (unbounded/negative semantics, overflow-safe `+`, `ite`, `distinct`,
  UF-over-Int → UF-over-BV) plus an all-bounded proof for `unsat` (or `sat`-only via
  replay). No such lowering exists today.
