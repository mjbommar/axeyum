# P1.4 — Incremental e-graph (congruence closure) — KEYSTONE

**Size:** L · **Depends on:** — · **Keystone:** unlocks most of Track 2 and P1.5/P1.6

## Goal
Build a standalone, backtrackable incremental e-graph (congruence closure) with
explanation tracking, as a new crate `axeyum-egraph` (or module). It is the shared
equality bus, the model substrate, and the proof forest for EUF, lazy arrays,
datatypes, arithmetic equality propagation, and **all** quantifier work.

## Why / leverage
Almost every eager→lazy theory upgrade in Track 2 is blocked on this. axeyum does
UF by Ackermann (quadratic, no equality sharing) and arrays by eager elimination;
neither can share congruence reasoning. A real e-graph is the keystone the
reference review identified as "do first."

## Design (port the modern Z3 `euf_egraph` design)
- **Signature table over argument roots** (one per `(decl, arity)`; unary/binary/
  comm/nary specializations); two nodes are congruent iff equal signature under
  the current union-find.
- **Two structures per node**: union-find (`root`, union-by-size) for membership,
  and a **separate proof forest** (`target` + `justification`) for explanations.
- **Deferred merge** via a worklist; on merge, remove/re-insert parents to drive
  the congruence cascade.
- **Explain to the LCA** (not the root); congruence justifications store a
  **timestamp**, premises recovered structurally.
- **Backtrackable** (trail of merges) to integrate with push/pop and the CDCL(T)
  loop.
- **Lifetime-free `Copy` handles** (axeyum rule) — node ids, not pointers/lifetimes.

## Phase exit criteria
- A standalone e-graph that: closes congruence incrementally, supports push/pop,
  produces minimal sound explanations, and carries theory-var lists.
- The **independent congruence checker** re-validates explanations (the EUF
  analogue of `check_drat`/Farkas — extends "trusted small checking" to equality).
- Property tests: random merge sequences agree with a brute-force union-find +
  congruence oracle; explanations are sound (the returned literals entail the
  equality) and acyclic.
