[`P2.5-nra/`](P2.5-nra/) detail set)

## One-paragraph thesis

axeyum decides only narrow nonlinear fragments today (single-variable polynomial
root isolation, even-power refutation, `x*x`, and a sound-incomplete McCormick /
branch-and-bound relaxation capped at ≤2 cross-products). Z3 and cvc5 are
*complete* on QF_NRA. We close the gap the way they did — **not** by jumping
straight to a full cylindrical algebraic decomposition, but by building a
**layered nonlinear engine**: a cheap incremental-linearization loop catches the
easy majority, interval constraint propagation prunes, and a complete
**NLSAT/MCSAT or Cylindrical-Algebraic-Coverings (CAC)** oracle is the decision
procedure of last resort for the decidable real fragment. Integers (NIA) ride the
same machinery via real relaxation + branch-and-bound, finishing with bounded
bit-blasting. The whole stack stays pure-Rust, deterministic, `unknown`-first, and
**DISAGREE=0** — every `sat` replays against the original term; every `unsat`
carries an independently re-derivable certificate or a trust-ledger entry.

## Why this, why now

- **Measured gap.** Our QF_NRA / QF_NIA decide-rate trails Z3 substantially
  ([decide-rate frontier](../decide-rate-frontier-2026-06-28.md),
  [gap analysis Gap 8](../gap-analysis-z3-cvc5-2026-06-22.md)). The residual
  hard instances on measured divisions (issue5836-2 = QF_UFNRA) are nonlinear.
- **It's a known 15-year catch-up, but a layered one.** Incremental linearization
  (the cheap tier) is responsible for a large fraction of practical wins and is
  weeks, not months. The complete oracle is the multi-month keystone — but it is
  *sliceable* (polynomial arithmetic → root isolation → algebraic numbers →
  projection → search), and each slice is independently testable.
- **Shared machinery pays twice.** The real-algebraic-number + polynomial core
  built here is exactly what NIA, quantifier elimination
  ([P2.6](P2.6-quantifiers.md)), and Positivstellensatz/SOS proof reconstruction
  (degree-2 SOS already reconstructs to kernel-checked Lean) reuse.

## Sequencing at a glance

```
Phase A (algebraic core) ──┬──> Phase B (incr. linearization)  [cheap, weeks]
                           │         │
                           │         v
                           ├──> Phase C (ICP filter)           [cheap]
                           │         │
                           │         v
                           └──> Phase D (NLSAT/CAC complete)    [keystone, months]
                                     │
                                     v
                              Phase E (NIA: relax+branch+blast) [reuses A–D]
```

Phase A is the long pole that everything else depends on; Phases B and C deliver
*measured* decide-rate before D lands; D is the completeness keystone; E reuses
all of it. See [02-architecture.md](P2.5-nra/02-architecture.md) for the
component-level DAG.

## Standing discipline (applies to every phase)

- **`unknown` is first-class.** Outside the decidable fragment / past the resource
  budget we return `unknown`, never a guess.
- **DISAGREE=0.** Every nonlinear `sat` is replay-checked against the original
  term (rational or `RealAlgebraic` witness); every `unsat` is either
  independently re-derivable or carries a trust-ledger entry. A z3-gated
  differential fuzz (`crates/axeyum-solver/tests/nra_differential_fuzz.rs`,
  `nia_differential_fuzz.rs`) gates every decider change.
- **Measured, not asserted.** No decide-rate claim without re-running the
  scoreboard ([bench-results/SCOREBOARD.md](../../../bench-results/SCOREBOARD.md)).
- **64 GB cap.** Heavy runs go through `scripts/mem-run.sh`; the multivariate
  blowup is exactly what OOMs the box.
