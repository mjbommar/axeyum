# P2.6 — Quantifiers (e-matching, triggers, MBQI, QE)

**Size:** L–XL · **Depends on:** [P1.4 e-graph](../track-1-engine/P1.4-egraph.md),
[P1.5 CDCL(T)](../track-1-engine/P1.5-cdcl-t-loop.md) · **Frontier**

## Goal
Bring quantifier reasoning from finite-domain + first-slice E-matching/MBQI to
production: a Matching Abstract Machine for incremental e-matching, real trigger
inference, lazy clause-form instantiation, MBQI completeness machinery, and
model-based projection for QE.

## Current checkpoint (2026-07-11)

The e-graph instantiation loop, single/multi-pattern trigger inference, MBQI/MBP
refutation, finite guarded-Int certificates, and targeted Euclidean-residue plus
affine-growth CEGQI are live. ADR-0096 gives restricted infinite-domain
`forall* exists` SAT an arena-stable affine Skolem recipe in `Model` and a
separate affine/reflexive original-assertion checker. ADR-0098 extends that
contract to one exact positive-`or` guarded unit-gap theorem.

The checked UNSAT ladder now includes exact Euclidean residue (ADR-0095),
positive-slope affine growth (ADR-0097), hierarchical nested XOR (ADR-0099),
evaluator-replayed closed universals (ADR-0100), exact finite equality
partitions (ADR-0101), and finite free-Boolean counterexample covers
(ADR-0108). ADR-0102 through ADR-0106 reconstruct the first five classes through
genuine quantifier elimination/application and kernel-checked integer or
propositional reasoning. ADR-0108 reconstructs its one-positive-universal-leaf
slice by applying every concrete source tuple and closing a bounded
excluded-middle tree; evaluator/search results guide construction but cannot
grant `False`.

The checked SAT ladder includes affine/reflexive `forall* exists` Skolem recipes
(ADR-0096), the exact guarded unit-gap witness (ADR-0098), and free-Boolean
models for positive Bool/Int universals (ADR-0107). The latter retains untouched
assertions, exactly lifts integer `ite`, and requires source-bound LIA-DPLL/DRAT
closure under the proposed model. Concrete counterexamples generalize only
search blocking cubes and never grant SAT.

ADR-0121 extends the same small-checker boundary to one exact BV identity
recipe: a same-width universal variable with coefficient one and zero offset.
After substitution, only reflexive `bvsle`/`bvule` (plus equality) is accepted;
modular arithmetic, composite atoms, offsets, and piecewise witnesses decline.

ADR-0110 lands the first general lazy clause-evaluation slice. E-matched
disjunctions of equality/disequality literals are evaluated in three values
against the matching e-graph plus recorded ground disequalities. True instances
are suppressed; all-false and one-undetermined complete source instances are
scheduled before unresolved/non-clausal fallback traffic. The public witness
API remains complete, and no bare literal is asserted without its false-sibling
justifications. A 256-match target reduces the first active batch to one and
improves median optimized batch-plus-QF time 4.237→2.524 ms (40.4%). The public
54-row quantified-BV division is decision-identical to baseline, and quantified
LIA remains 12/12. Incremental MAM matching and online detached-literal
justifications remain the next engine depth.

ADR-0111 lands T2.6.1's first shared-state/compiler slice. One refutation
attempt now infers triggers once, interns identical recursive patterns, extends
one ground bridge only with appended source instances, and executes all unique
patterns against one batched class/application index per round. A
32-quantifier/256-term target preserves all 8,192 ordered tuples while improving
optimized median matching 17.477→0.974 ms (17.9x). A retained two-round chain
replays `Unsat`, the 54-row quantified-BV division is decision-identical, and
quantified LIA remains 12/12. The full MAM exit remains open: bytecode,
inverted-parent paths, relevance/generation filtering, and direct add/merge
candidate propagation must replace round rematching.

ADR-0112 lands the add-only candidate-propagation slice. A revision-checked
e-match index extends class/application maps from the new node suffix while the
retained session maps trigger-root declarations to cached complete pattern
matches. An add-only round rematches only roots that gained applications; a real
merge or pop invalidates root-keyed indexes and conservatively rematches all
patterns. On 64 unrelated roots and 4,096 retained applications, appending one
application executes 1 rather than 64 patterns, preserves every complete tuple,
and improves optimized median second-round matching 2.555→0.311 ms (8.2x).
Selective merge propagation still requires inverted parent paths;
relevance/generation filtering remains open and bytecode remains measurement
gated against the recursive compiled matcher.

ADR-0113 lands selective merge propagation. Every real union, including
congruence cascades, enters an index-consumed journal; normal merge refreshes no
longer rebuild the graph. The session follows transitive e-class parent links
from changed equality endpoints and rematches only reached trigger roots, while
cached substitutions and multi-pattern joins compare current roots. Raw
applications are retained so explicitly equal `f(a)`/`f(b)` terms still yield
both sound bindings when `a` and `b` remain unequal. A complete-round target
with 64 roots and 4,096 applications executes 1 instead of 64 patterns and
improves optimized median merge-round time 2.231→0.151 ms (14.8x). Exact
path-shape filtering and relevance/generation controls remain before the MAM
exit; bytecode remains measurement-gated.

ADR-0114 lands exact declaration/argument path shape. Every interned pattern
occurrence contributes its child-to-root steps to one shared flat trie; merge
lookup pairs e-class roots with trie states and follows only compatible parent
arguments, with cycle-safe visited pairs and sorted/deduplicated terminals. Add
queues and current-root joins remain unchanged. A target with 64 patterns
sharing one outer declaration and 4,096 applications reaches one divergent
nested path, executes 1 instead of 64 patterns, and improves optimized median
complete-round time 12.777→0.386 ms (33.1x).

ADR-0115 adds exact semantic path filters. E-class roots retain backtrackable
sorted declaration sets; terminals require the changed start class to contain
the expected nested declaration, and transitions may require one direct
nullary ground sibling to be equal modulo the e-graph. Compound ground siblings
remain unfiltered. On 64 same-shape patterns and 4,096 applications, unfiltered,
class-only, ground-only, and combined modes reach 64, 8, 8, and 1 terminals and
run in optimized medians of 13.453, 2.314, 1.991, and 0.404 ms. The combined
route is 33.3x faster while returning identical tuples. ADR-0116 evaluates the
remaining relevance/generation boundary; bytecode remains measurement-gated.

ADR-0116 replaces affected-pattern rescans with exact top-application deltas.
Initial matching remains complete; later add rounds queue new root applications,
and merge rounds queue filtered path-terminal applications. Matches append to a
monotonic cache and joins canonicalize current roots. One affected pattern over
4,096 outer applications scans 4,096 versus 1 top application and improves the
optimized complete-round median 0.370→0.122 ms (3.03x) with identical tuples.
All retained bridge terms are active-source relevant by construction, so a
separate relevance bit would currently be a no-op. Generation-cost scheduling
and bytecode remain independently measurement-gated.

ADR-0117 lands the first checked detached-literal route. A public certificate
binds an exact source universal instance to one remaining equality/disequality
literal and every false sibling's named original-ground reasons. A batch checker
reconstructs the instance in a fresh source context and separately replays each
reason subset before the literal enters QF search. Generated-premise reasons
decline to the complete source instance. On 128 six-sibling matches, query DAG
nodes fall 4,230→2,438 and tree nodes 10,121→4,745; optimized median QF time
falls 8.250→3.226 ms and checked end-to-end time 11.301→9.886 ms. That
source-only boundary left recursive provenance, non-equality theory literals,
proof serialization, and direct online SAT insertion open.

ADR-0118 composes that checked implication across rounds. Every admitted
generated equality/disequality retains either an exact universal-instance
certificate or the prior checked propagation that concluded it. A bounded
recursive checker requires an exact sorted table for all non-source named
reasons, reconstructs every substitution, and rejects missing/duplicate/unused,
reordered, wrong-variant/conclusion, nested-tampered, over-depth, or over-node
artifacts. A six-stage chain preserves UNSAT while query DAG nodes fall 54→17
and tree nodes 117→33.

ADR-0119 reuses those checked implications in one retained CDCL(T)+EUF
session. The original ground Boolean/equality skeleton is encoded once; each
batch backtracks to level zero, appends any new equality atoms in root scope,
and adds only exact-instance or recursively checked clauses. Learned clauses,
activities, and phases survive matching rounds. Online SAT only resumes
matching, and online UNSAT still requires an ordinary QF refutation of the exact
admitted ground set. A six-stage target cuts complete QF rebuilds 7→2 and
improves five-run optimized median time 0.560→0.351 ms (37.3%, 1.60x).
Non-equality antecedents, online proof serialization, and SAT-trail-driven
matching callbacks remain open.

ADR-0120 closes the measured candidate-equality boundary without adding a hot
assignment callback. At an ordinary matching fixpoint, true equality atoms from
the retained SAT candidate are merged in one temporary matching-e-graph scope.
Exact merge paths execute only affected patterns, a reverse index joins only
their quantifiers, and concrete tuples are materialized before pop. Candidate
equalities never become reasons or evidence; only complete exact source
instances enter ADR-0119 and product UNSAT still requires QF replay. A nested
trigger target moves `Unknown` to `Unsat` and improves optimized median time
0.573→0.148 ms (3.87x). A 64-pattern target scans 1 pattern/application,
returns the full-scan tuple, and improves median 5.478→4.329 ms (1.27x).
High-frequency assignment callbacks remain measurement-gated.

The remaining public unknowns are both large nested/alternating UNSAT formulas.
`small-pipeline-fixpoint-3` is the next frontier at 235 DAG nodes versus
`bug802` at 3,317. Its theorem is a bounded hardware-state fixpoint implication:
three concrete transition steps must equal one of three existentially copied
prefix states. The next increment must derive a checked finite-state/pigeonhole
or transition-replay refutation from the original closure, not enumerate the
full 32-bit binder product.

## Phase exit criteria
- MAM-based e-matching + trigger inference + lazy clause evaluation decide
  quantified instances axeyum currently returns `unknown` on, with behavior (and
  `unknown` rate) approaching Z3's heuristics; models replay-checked.
- Every new quantified UNSAT route has an independently checkable artifact, and
  every infinite-domain SAT route carries a model/certificate that checks the
  original quantified assertion rather than bypassing replay.
