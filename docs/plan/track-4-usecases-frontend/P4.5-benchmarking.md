# P4.5 — Benchmarking & the performance gate

**Size:** M · **Depends on:** — · **DO FIRST.** No Track 1 performance phase is
"done" without this. **Front:** performance (load-bearing #1)

## Goal
A reproducible, committed **head-to-head harness**: axeyum vs Z3 on a fixed small
QF_BV slice, reporting PAR-2 and the sat/unsat/unknown split, with soundness
cross-checks (oracle agreement, model replay). This is the measuring instrument
the whole performance effort is judged by.

## Why / leverage
The audit found the binding gap is the *absence of a clean measured head-to-head*:
the strongest data points are "~3× slower on one shared hard instance" and "~90%
decided on an easy family **with the oracle disabled**." Until each Track 1 change
is measured against Z3 on the same committed slice, "performance parity" is
unsubstantiated. Must respect the memory rule: **never sweep the 41GB corpus** —
curate a small, committed, representative slice and measure once per change.

## Phase exit criteria
- A single committed command runs axeyum vs Z3 (oracle enabled) on a fixed slice
  and reports PAR-2 + decided split + soundness cross-checks, with a baseline and a
  diff-vs-baseline regression view. Every subsequent Track 1 change reports its
  delta through this harness in STATUS.md / `bench-results/`.
