# P4.6 — CHC / Horn solving (PDR/Spacer-style unbounded reachability)

**Size:** XL · **Depends on:** [P3.8 interpolation](../track-3-proof-lean/P3.8-interpolation.md),
[P1.5 CDCL(T)](../track-1-engine/P1.5-cdcl-t-loop.md),
[P2.6 quantifiers (MBP)](../track-2-theories/P2.6-quantifiers.md) ·
**Substantial direct-API seed; production-depth frontier**

## Goal
Add a **Constrained Horn Clause** solver: given a set of CHCs over a background
theory (LIA/LRA/BV/arrays), either find an interpretation of the uninterpreted
predicates that satisfies all clauses (`sat` → an inductive invariant / safe) or
derive a ground refutation (`unsat` → a counterexample-to-induction trace). This
is the engine for **unbounded** program verification — the thing BMC and
k-induction (already in `capabilities.rs`) only approximate.

## Why / leverage
This is the single biggest *categorical* hole versus Z3. Z3's **Spacer** (the
default `fixedpoint` engine, `muz/spacer`) makes Z3 the de-facto backend for
SeaHorn, the Rust/C verification frontends, and most CHC-based tooling. axeyum
today can do **bounded** model checking and **inductive** (k-induction) proving,
but cannot *discover* an inductive invariant — so it returns `Inconclusive` on
exactly the non-inductive safety properties that matter most. CHC closes that.

It is also the natural consumer of two other new pieces: **interpolation**
(P3.8) supplies the lemmas that generalize a blocked counterexample, and
**model-based projection** (P2.6) supplies the existential-quantifier elimination
PDR needs. Building CHC forces those to exist and pays for them.

## Design notes
- **Front end:** accept CHCs both via the SMT-LIB `(rule …)`/`(query …)`
  fixedpoint surface and as a direct `HornClause` IR (head predicate +
  constrained body), so the symexec/CFG frontend (P4.2) and `bounded_model_check`
  can lower to it.
- **Engine:** IC3/PDR adapted to SMT (Spacer): a trace of frames
  (over-approximations per predicate), proof obligations, inductive
  generalization, and **lemma reuse**. Reachability is driven by the warm
  incremental solver (P1.5/ADR-0009); blocked cubes are generalized by
  interpolation (P3.8) and MBP (P2.6).
- **Soundness contract (non-negotiable):** `safe`/`sat` must carry the discovered
  invariant and re-check it (each clause is theory-valid under the
  interpretation — a finite set of independent `unsat` checks); `unsat` must carry
  the replay-checked ground derivation (a finite resolution/BMC trace), reusing
  the existing counterexample-trace machinery. `unknown` (divergence / budget) is
  first-class, never a wrong `safe`.

## Current status

This is no longer a green-field engine. The public `HornSystem`/`HornClause`/
`solve_horn` boundary handles Real and Bool/BV state, stratified multi-predicate
systems, lower-stratum nonlinear-body folding, and sort-compatible mutually
recursive SCCs through PDR/IMC. Candidate safe interpretations are rechecked
against every source clause; unsafe outcomes retain replay-checked reachability.
The [2026-07-21 categorical-engine audit](../categorical-engine-depth-audit-2026-07-21.md)
records 22/22 focused tests passing under a hard 4 GiB cap.

The real residual is explicit: no SMT-LIB `declare-rel`/`rule`/`query`, no
committed CHC-vs-Spacer corpus, no Int/array/mixed-state dispatch, no incompatible-
signature SCC merge, no genuine nonlinear recursion, and no serialized
`CertifiedChcSafe` bundle. Those gaps keep the phase WIP and far from production
Spacer parity without making the engine absent.

## Phase exit criteria
- A CHC system over LIA/LRA is solved both ways: `safe` returns an inductive
  invariant that re-checks (every clause theory-valid under it), `unsafe` returns
  a replay-checked ground trace; on a committed CHC slice the decide-rate is
  measured head-to-head vs Z3/Spacer ([P4.5](P4.5-benchmarking.md)).
- BV and array constraint theories supported (arrays via P2.2 lazy ROW).
- `unknown` (divergence, budget) stays first-class; no wrong `safe` under the
  soundness-negative + differential gates.
