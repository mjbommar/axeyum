# P6.0 — Kernel trustworthiness

**Size:** M · **Depends on:** — · **Unconditional**

## Goal
Make `axeyum-lean-kernel` trustworthy **by adversarial test** rather than by
assertion, and close the deferrals that are deferred *by permission*.

## Why / leverage
This phase is owed **whether or not Track 6 proceeds**. P3.6 and P3.7 route all
their assurance through this component, and every `Lean unsat` claim in
`bench-results/DOMINANCE.md` rests on it.

On 2026-07-15 the trusted admission gate accepted `theorem bad : False`
([`../research/09-P0-kernel-unsoundness.md`](../research/09-P0-kernel-unsoundness.md);
fixed in `d26ad887`, ADR-0165). ADR-0036:50-52 states the obligation — "the entire
value is that this core accepts exactly what Lean's kernel does" — and
ADR-0036:62-65 names the risk — "a wrong type-checker would wrongly accept
proofs." **The obligation was stated and never tested negatively.**

The incident produced a rule that generalizes beyond the kernel and is the real
deliverable of this phase:

> **Deferral by rejection is safe. Deferral by permission is not.**

Every other item on `inductive.rs`'s deferred list errors explicitly. The one that
was silently *allowed* was the one that was unsound — and it was filed as a
completeness gap. Two further instances are already known (T6.0.2, T6.0.4), which
is why this is a phase and not a patch.

Corroborating, from [`../research/01-itp-anatomy.md`](../research/01-itp-anatomy.md):
every historical Lean/Rocq kernel bug lived at a **feature seam**, and
example-based testing appears **nowhere** in the record of how they were found.
The kernel began this track with 181 hand-written tests and **zero fuzz**. Both
initial findings came from *reading the code against the metatheory*. T6.0.3 now
adds the first generated seam gate; this historical baseline remains the reason
the gate is mandatory rather than evidence that testing is sufficient.

## Sizing correction (note 06 addendum)
**Nested inductives and well-founded recursion are not kernel work** — Lean compiles
both away before its kernel sees them; earlier estimates over-charged ~1200–1900
LoC. WF recursion's real kernel content is `Acc.rec`, i.e. reflexive fields. And
**recursive-indexed + reflexive/higher-order fields are one work item** (both
generalize the IH to `Π telescope, motive (f args)`).

**The real spine: positivity checker → (recursive-indexed + reflexive) → mutual.**
Positivity first is not a preference — today's rejection of the later gaps is what
is incidentally enforcing it (T6.0.2).

The middle node is now concretely preregistered by
[accepted ADR-0353](../../research/09-decisions/adr-0353-preregister-lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses.md)
and the
[TL2.12 execution plan](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-tl2.12-plan-2026-07-22.md):
every supported recursive field `u : Pi xs, I params indices` receives exactly
`Pi xs, motive indices (u xs)`, and its computation rule supplies the matching
recursive call. Direct recursion is the empty-telescope/empty-index control;
mutual remains the next atomic widening.
[M0](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-m0-2026-07-22.md) now
freezes the exact source/wire/metadata contract with no Axeyum product
observation. The
[M1 result](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-m1-2026-07-22.md)
routes direct recursion through the shared representation with exact
declaration/computation identity. The
[M2 result](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-m2-2026-07-22.md)
now admits the complete native matrix through that representation, rejects ten
native mutation classes, and repeats 768 recursive profiles plus the retained
840-case positivity population. The
[M3 result](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-m3-2026-07-22.md)
completes both official construct targets twice with exact recursor comparison,
closes metadata/publication mutations, and admits the pre-elaborated
well-founded row through `Acc.rec` without claiming frontend support. The
[M4 result](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-m4-2026-07-22.md)
now separately confirms pinned Lean and Axeyum computation twice at the exact
Vector/Acc normal forms and regenerates the current machine assurance matrix.
The [M5 result](../../plan/lean-recursive-induction-hypotheses-final-2026-07-22.md)
closes every bounded gate and marks TL2.12 DONE; mutual is the next semantic
widening.

That widening is now preregistered by
[proposed ADR-0354](../../research/09-decisions/adr-0354-preregister-lean-mutual-inductive-groups.md)
and the
[TL2.13 plan](../../plan/lean-mutual-inductive-groups-tl2.13-plan-2026-07-22.md).
The soundness-critical change is the admission unit: one ordered group shares
parameters, a complete positivity occurrence set, all motives, and all minors;
each recursive field selects its terminal family's motive and recursor; every
recursor self-checks before atomic publication. A singleton must reproduce all
existing identities and behavior. The
[M0 result](../../plan/lean-mutual-inductive-groups-m0-2026-07-22.md) now
freezes explicit non-indexed and indexed cross-family official computations
before product work, including the distinction between semantic family order
and dependency-ordered wire recursor arrays. M1 group representation and
singleton delegation are next; TL2.14 remains a separate frontend phase.

## Phase exit criteria
- Every kernel deferral **rejects** rather than permits, with a recorded error variant.
- Positivity has a real checker, landed **before** the inductive gaps it guards.
- A `kernel accepts False` fuzz class runs in `just check`. **DONE for every
  currently representable seam; extend it with each TL2 admission.**
- The prelude-axiom boundary is decided by ADR and published — not inherited.
- A kernel performance baseline exists.
- The preludes are namespaced and coexist in one `Kernel` without panicking.
- `lean_pp` round-trips (`parse(print(t)) == t`) — the cross-check checks what we proved.

## The verification boundary (as of this phase)
- **In the TCB:** the kernel, the checkers, 65 undischarged prelude assumptions,
  `rustc`, **6 of 14** open ledger entries (`ArrayElim`, `Ackermann`, `DatatypeElim`,
  `Fpa2Bv`, plus `IntBlast`/`XorGaussian` — "unsound with no recovery" at
  pedantic 3).
- **Not in the TCB:** the solver, the translator, the search, any agent.
- **Not covered:** `sat` results (see P6.1c); anything outside the stated fragment.

## Why this ships regardless
Nothing in this phase depends on a goal layer existing. If Track 6 is abandoned
tomorrow, P3.6/P3.7 still need every task here. **This is the one phase with no
decision attached** — and it is worth noting that the track paid for itself here
before writing a line of prover code, though the P0 was found by *reading the
kernel against its metatheory*, which a plain hardening task does more cheaply.
That is Track 3's credit, not this track's.
