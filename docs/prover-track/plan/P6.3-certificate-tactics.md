# P6.3 — Certificate-first tactics

**Size:** L, sliced per tactic · **Depends on:** P6.2 · Each tactic lands alone

## Goal
A tactic is **not** a function that builds a proof term (de Bruijn) nor a function
over an abstract `thm` (LCF). It is an **untrusted procedure that emits a
certificate**, plus a **small checker** that turns the certificate into a
kernel-checked term.

## Why / leverage
This is reconstruction's shape, generalized from certificates about *formulas* to
certificates about *goals*. It is the one design where our existing automation is
an asset rather than a liability: **every decision procedure becomes a tactic for
free.**

Independent validation: **Metamath Zero's producer/consumer split is this thesis**,
stated by the person who thought hardest about it
([`../research/11-dedukti-and-substrates.md`](../research/11-dedukti-and-substrates.md)).
Two of its consequences are design constraints here — **binary certificates, not
text**, and **checking throughput as a defended gate** (set.mm: ZFC, <200 ms).

The standing constraint from the same note: **don't build the universal thing,
build the bridge someone wants.** Each certificate format below must serve **one
consumer we can name**.

**The template is already written, by someone else.** Rocq's `lia` (Micromega) is
*untrusted search + a reflective checker + a certificate* — our identity sentence
in another codebase. Cite it; do not reinvent it.

## Slice order
`T6.3.1 → T6.3.2 → T6.3.3 → T6.3.4`, with T6.3.6 independent and T6.3.5 blocked on
being scoped. Each lands alone; none is justified by a later one.

## Phase exit criteria
- An obligation **P3.7 could not touch** — because no Lean is present and the goal
  needed decomposing — is discharged by an untrusted tactic emitting a
  kernel-checked certificate.
- `counterexample` never ships before P6.1c.
- Instability is measured and ratcheted.

## The open premise this phase inherits
**Certificate-first is a *checking* discipline. It presumes a certificate exists to
emit.** `decide` is safe — the solver already found the proof, so the certificate
is a transcript. **`simp`, `induction`, and `invariant` are not**: they must *find*
the decomposition first, and nothing in the design says how.

That is [`P6.6-paper-attempt.md`](../process/quantified-uf-probe.md)'s question, and it is
still open — the *evidence* previously offered for it (quantified UF 0/5) turned
out to measure our plumbing rather than the fragment, but the question did not go
away with it.

## The verification boundary (added by this phase)
- **Newly in the TCB:** one checker per certificate format. Each must be small,
  independently testable, and adversarially tested — the field's measured lesson is
  that the trusted parts that aren't small are where the bugs live.
- **Not in the TCB:** every tactic. That is the point.
