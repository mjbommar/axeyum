# P6.1 ‚Äî The obligation bridge (CIC ‚áÑ IR)

**Size:** XL as a monolith; **sliced a/b/c/d** ¬∑ **Depends on:** P6.0 ¬∑
**The crux**

## Goal
A translation between the kernel's CIC `Expr` and `axeyum-ir`'s `TermNode`, in
both directions, over a **published fragment**, with soundness gates for `unsat`
*and* `sat`.

## Why / leverage
Everything above this is decoration if it fails, which is why it is first after
hardening. It is also where the plan is cheapest to be wrong.

The mismatch is structural, not incidental
([`../research/08-solver-automation-assets.md`](../research/08-solver-automation-assets.md)):

- `axeyum_ir::TermNode` (`term.rs:344`): **no binders**; a **closed `Op` enum**
  (`term.rs:78`), so an application's head is an enum variant, not a term; a
  **closed, non-dependent `Sort`** (`sort.rs:121`).
- `axeyum_lean_kernel::ExprNode` (`expr.rs:80`): `Lam`/`Pi`/`Let`, de Bruijn,
  `Sort(LevelId)`, universes.
- **The kernel's `[dependencies]` is empty.** It has never heard of the IR. Both
  intern to lifetime-free `Copy` handles ‚Äî that is house style ("mirroring
  `axeyum-ir`", `lean-kernel/src/lib.rs:11-15`), **not a bridge**.
- `grep -rE "fn .*\(.*ExprId.*\).*-> (Result<)?TermId"` ‚Üí **zero matches.** The
  needed direction has never been written.

## The asymmetry that makes this tractable
**`unsat` is sound by construction.** The translation is untrusted, but the
resulting proof term is kernel-checked, so a mistranslation yields a *failure*,
never a wrong theorem. This is the same shape as `verify_invariant`
(`pdr.rs:835-876`) ‚Äî the pattern worth stealing wholesale.

**`sat` is not**, and this is the single most important unmitigated risk found by
the track. A mistranslated goal returning `sat` hands back a **confident wrong
counterexample with nothing checking it** ‚Äî and counterexamples are the
differentiator. Hence P6.1c is a soundness gate, not polish.

## Slice order and why
`T6.1a ‚Üí T6.1b ‚Üí T6.1c ‚Üí T6.1d`. Each lands alone:

- **a** ships as a reconstruction refactor. Zero new capability, and that is the
  point: it answers "does the seam exist?" for the cost of a refactor.
- **b** makes `decide` (T6.3.1) possible ‚Äî the first real capability.
- **c** makes counterexamples trustworthy. **Before c, `counterexample` must not
  ship.**
- **d** closes a soundness hole that exists today and is independent of the rest.

## The open risk, stated plainly
[`../research/07-reconstruction-assets.md`](../research/07-reconstruction-assets.md)
found that reconstruction is **ad-hoc**: `scan_proof_fragment` (`:1839`) matches
~40 bespoke `ProofFragment` shapes ‚Äî including `TwoByteMemcpy`, `BinarySearch16`,
`FifoBc04` (`:1409-1428`), named after individual benchmarks ‚Äî and replays a
recipe. Its control flow "runs backward from the answer"; a bridge's runs forward
from the question. `fresh_local_fvar` (`:7921-7930`) notes reconstruction
"otherwise builds closed terms" ‚Äî it never represents *not yet knowing*.

**If that cannot be extracted, T6.1a is a fiction and the slice plan's opening
move fails.** That is exactly why it is first: it is the cheapest place in the
whole track to discover the design is wrong.

**Stop condition:** T6.1a cannot be extracted into a reusable bridge ‚Üí the seam
does not exist ‚Üí stop. Cost: one refactor's worth of work, and reconstruction is
better documented either way.

## Phase exit criteria
- A published translatable fragment, with out-of-fragment goals declining.
- Sound `unsat` **and** `sat` gates.
- A fuzz seed-class per divergent totality convention.
- A coverage number on real obligations.

## The verification boundary (added by this phase)
- **Newly in the TCB:** nothing. The translator is untrusted by design; the kernel
  gate covers `unsat` and T6.1c covers `sat`.
- **Still not covered:** anything outside the published fragment ‚Äî which is why
  the fragment is *published* and declines rather than approximates.
