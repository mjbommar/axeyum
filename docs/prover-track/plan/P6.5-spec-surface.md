# P6.5 — Definitions and specs

**Size:** L · **Depends on:** P6.3 · **May correctly reduce to P5.2 — test first**

## Goal
Only what software verification requires: recursive definitions, refinement
relations, contracts.

## Why this phase is the danger zone
Note 04's graveyard — **ESC/Java, Spec#, VCC, Code Contracts** — died of one cause:
**assuming the spec is free.** The middle is occupied only by (a) controlling the
language (Dafny, SPARK), (b) having a regulator (DO-178C, FIPS), or (c) abandoning
soundness (Infer survived by *leaving*). **We do none of the three.**

And *Verification Theatre* (ePrint 2026/192) is what failure looks like when you
get the proofs right: **13 vulnerabilities escaped verification in libcrux/hpke-rs
— four *inside* verified code**, including a false serialization proof — diagnosed
as the **undocumented verification boundary**. The proofs were fine; nobody could
tell what they covered.

## Test the reduction before building
**P5.2 already plans contracts** (`#[requires]`/`#[ensures]`, status TODO), and is
explicitly anti-deductive: *"Contracts keep every obligation finite and decidable —
which is what separates this from ghost-code deductive systems"*
(`P5.2-contracts-modular.md:16-18`), with Verus named and its deductive lane
"explicitly not copied."

**If P5.2 suffices, cancel this phase and end the track at P6.4.** The only thing
P6.5 adds is what P5.2 declines — recursion (`P5.2:33`, "recursion declined
honestly in v1") and refinement. **Measure whether anyone needs it before building
it.**

## Phase exit criteria
- The P5.2 reduction is **tested**, not assumed.
- Spec cost is measured and published.
- Every artifact ships its boundary.

## The most likely honest outcome
**This phase is cancelled.** P5.2 + P6.3's `decide` may cover the obligations
anyone actually has, and the graveyard's lesson is that the marginal value of
"more expressive specs" is negative when the spec is the bottleneck. **Do T6.5.0
and be willing to stop.**
